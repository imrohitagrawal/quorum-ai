# Personas

## P-001: High-Stakes Knowledge Worker

### Profile

A public user such as an analyst, strategist, founder, consultant, researcher, or professional who uses AI to explore important decisions and wants stronger confidence than a single model can provide.

### Goals

- Cross-check answers across multiple frontier models.
- Identify where answers agree, disagree, or omit important context.
- See source links for credibility.
- Reduce hallucination risk before using AI output in important work.

### Pain Points

- Manually copies the same prompt into several chatbots.
- Spends time reconciling conflicting responses.
- Cannot easily tell which answer is better supported.
- Worries that a single model may be biased, incomplete, outdated, or overconfident.

### Success Looks Like

The user can submit one query, review model-specific evidence, understand disagreement, and leave with a sourced synthesis that is explicitly framed as decision support.

## P-002: Research-Oriented Learner

### Profile

A student, independent researcher, or curious user doing complex research where current information and source support matter.

### Goals

- Compare explanations across model styles.
- Understand uncertainty and disagreement.
- Use citations to continue independent research.

### Pain Points

- Search results and chatbot answers are fragmented.
- Model answers can sound confident even when source support is weak.
- Manual comparison interrupts learning flow.

### Success Looks Like

The user gets a clear view of consensus, disagreement, source-backed claims, and follow-up areas to investigate.

## P-003: Creative Strategist

### Profile

A marketer, founder, writer, or product thinker who wants multiple model perspectives for brainstorming and strategy.

### Goals

- Use different model strengths in one workflow.
- Compare divergent ideas.
- Synthesize a more complete final direction.

### Pain Points

- Creative outputs vary widely across tools.
- It is hard to compare quality and usefulness manually.
- Valuable disagreements are easy to miss.

### Success Looks Like

The user sees varied model outputs, critique between models, and a concise final synthesis that preserves useful differences.

## Operator Persona: Product/Support Owner

### Responsibilities

- Maintain warning language and model defaults.
- Monitor cost per query and failures by provider/model.
- Handle user confusion around sources, high-stakes warnings, and unsupported model behavior.

### Success Looks Like

The product remains understandable, cost-aware, safe for public use, and honest about limitations.
